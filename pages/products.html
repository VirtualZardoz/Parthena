Copper

Copper is one of the most versatile and widely used metals in the world, prized for its excellent electrical conductivity, corrosion resistance, and thermal properties. As a leading copper trader, Swiss Metal Trading sources high-quality copper from major producing regions and supplies it to industrial clients worldwide.

Specifications & Grades

Grade Copper Content Form Primary Applications
Grade A Copper Cathodes 99.99% min Cathodes Electrical wire, cables, electronics
Copper Concentrate 20-40% Concentrate Refining into cathodes
Copper Blister 98-99% Blister Further refining
Copper Scrap Varies Scrap Recycling, secondary production

Sourcing Regions

We source copper primarily from:

  • South America: Chile and Peru (world's largest copper producers)
  • Africa: Democratic Republic of Congo and Zambia
  • Other Regions: Australia, Indonesia, and North America

Quality Standards

All copper products traded by Swiss Metal Trading meet international quality standards, including:

  • London Metal Exchange (LME) Grade A specifications
  • ASTM B115 standards for copper cathodes
  • ISO 431:1981 for copper refinery shapes

Technical Information

Key properties of copper that make it valuable for industrial applications:

  • Excellent electrical conductivity (second only to silver)
  • Superior thermal conductivity
  • High corrosion resistance
  • Good ductility and malleability
  • Antimicrobial properties
  • Recyclable without loss of performance

Aluminum

Aluminum is the most abundant metallic element in the Earth's crust and one of the most versatile metals in industrial applications. Valued for its lightweight properties, corrosion resistance, and recyclability, aluminum plays a crucial role in transportation, packaging, construction, and numerous other sectors. Swiss Metal Trading supplies high-quality aluminum products to meet diverse industrial needs.

Specifications & Grades

Grade Aluminum Content Form Primary Applications
Primary Aluminum Ingots 99.7% min Ingots Casting, extrusion, rolling
Aluminum Billets 99.7% min Billets Extrusion, forging
Aluminum Wire Rod 99.7% min Wire Rod Electrical applications, cables
Aluminum Scrap Varies Scrap Recycling, secondary production

Sourcing Regions

We source aluminum from key producing regions including:

  • Africa: Guinea (bauxite), South Africa
  • South America: Brazil, Jamaica
  • Other Regions: Australia, Middle East, Russia, China

Quality Standards

All aluminum products traded by Swiss Metal Trading meet international quality standards, including:

  • London Metal Exchange (LME) specifications
  • Aluminum Association standards
  • ASTM B209 for aluminum sheet and plate
  • EN 573 European standards for aluminum alloys

Technical Information

Key properties of aluminum that make it valuable for industrial applications:

  • Low density (approximately one-third the weight of steel)
  • Excellent corrosion resistance due to natural oxide layer
  • Good electrical and thermal conductivity
  • High reflectivity
  • Non-magnetic and non-sparking
  • Highly recyclable with minimal quality loss
  • Versatile alloying capabilities

Zinc

Zinc is a vital industrial metal primarily used for galvanizing steel to prevent corrosion, as well as in the production of brass, bronze, and other alloys. It also serves important roles in die-casting, chemical compounds, and various manufacturing processes. Swiss Metal Trading supplies high-quality zinc products to meet the needs of diverse industrial applications.

Specifications & Grades

Grade Zinc Content Form Primary Applications
Special High Grade (SHG) 99.995% min Ingots, Slabs Die-casting, brass production, galvanizing
High Grade (HG) 99.95% min Ingots, Slabs Galvanizing, brass production
Zinc Alloys Varies Ingots Die-casting, specialized applications
Zinc Concentrate 45-55% Concentrate Refining into zinc metal

Sourcing Regions

We source zinc from key producing regions including:

  • South America: Peru, Bolivia, Mexico
  • Asia: China, India
  • Other Regions: Australia, Canada, Europe

Quality Standards

All zinc products traded by Swiss Metal Trading meet international quality standards, including:

  • London Metal Exchange (LME) Special High Grade specifications
  • ASTM B6 for zinc metal
  • EN 1179 European standard for zinc and zinc alloys

Technical Information

Key properties of zinc that make it valuable for industrial applications:

  • Excellent corrosion protection for steel (galvanizing)
  • Good castability for die-casting applications
  • Low melting point (419.5°C)
  • Moderate strength and ductility
  • Good alloying properties with copper (brass) and other metals
  • Essential nutrient for human health and plant growth
  • Recyclable without loss of properties

Nickel

Nickel is a versatile metal known for its corrosion resistance, high-temperature strength, and alloying capabilities. It plays a crucial role in stainless steel production, which accounts for approximately two-thirds of global nickel consumption. Nickel is also increasingly important in battery technology for electric vehicles and renewable energy storage. Swiss Metal Trading supplies high-quality nickel products to meet diverse industrial needs.

Specifications & Grades

Grade Nickel Content Form Primary Applications
Primary Nickel (Class I) 99.8% min Cathodes, Briquettes, Pellets Stainless steel, superalloys, plating
Ferronickel 15-40% Ingots, Granules Stainless steel production
Nickel Sulfate 22% (Ni equivalent) Crystals, Solution Battery cathode materials, electroplating
Nickel Concentrate 8-15% Concentrate Refining into nickel metal

Sourcing Regions

We source nickel from key producing regions including:

  • Asia Pacific: Indonesia, Philippines, New Caledonia
  • Americas: Canada, Brazil
  • Other Regions: Russia, Australia, Africa

Quality Standards

All nickel products traded by Swiss Metal Trading meet international quality standards, including:

  • London Metal Exchange (LME) specifications
  • ASTM B39 for nickel
  • ISO 6283 for nickel cathodes
  • Battery-grade specifications for nickel sulfate

Technical Information

Key properties of nickel that make it valuable for industrial applications:

  • Excellent corrosion resistance, especially in alkaline environments
  • High-temperature strength and stability
  • Ferromagnetic properties
  • Good ductility and malleability
  • Catalytic properties
  • Essential component in stainless steel (300 series)
  • Critical material for lithium-ion battery cathodes
  • Recyclable from various waste streams

Quality Assurance & Certification

At Swiss Metal Trading, quality assurance is integral to our operations. We implement rigorous quality control measures throughout our supply chain to ensure all metal commodities meet international standards and client specifications.

Our quality assurance program includes:

  • Pre-shipment Inspection: Comprehensive inspection of products at origin to verify quality and quantity
  • Laboratory Testing: Chemical analysis and physical testing to confirm product specifications
  • Documentation Verification: Thorough review of certificates of analysis and origin
  • Third-Party Certification: Independent verification by accredited inspection agencies
  • Traceability Systems: Tracking of products from source to delivery

All products are accompanied by appropriate certification and documentation, including certificates of analysis, certificates of origin, and quality assurance reports as required by clients and regulatory authorities.
